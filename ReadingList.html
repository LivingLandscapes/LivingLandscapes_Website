Lab Reading List

This list is a starting point and meant to give all lab students common knowledge base. These are not the only readings you should do. Reading papers and relevant books should be an ongoing priority during your graduate program. Be curious, read widely, and have fun!

Foundational papers

Non-scientific literature

These are absolutely optional (and non-scientfic) readings. However, they have inspired my thinking and science, and I list them in case you may also enjoy them. Listed in no particular order:

  • Rogers, P. (2005). Firekeeper: selected poems. Rev. and expanded. Minneapolis, Minn., Milkweed Editions.
  • Williams, T. T. (2019). Erosion: essays of undoing. Sarah Crichton Books. ISBN: 9780374280062.
  • McPhee, J. (1990). Encounters with the archdruid. New York, Noonday Press.
  • McPhee, J. (1989). The control of nature. New York, Farrar, Straus, Giroux.
  • Atwood, M. (2013). Oryx And Crake. Virago Press.
  • Dillard, A. (1992). The Living. Harper Collins.
  • Berry, W. (2010). What are people for?: essays. Berkeley, CA, Counterpoint.
  • Dillard, A. (1984). Holy the firm. Harper and Row, NY.
